CROSS-OBSERVATION NOTES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. The marginal guarantee is not a subgroup guarantee. Coverage = 0.895
   overall, but 0.950 on accepted cases and 0.767 on rejected cases at
   30% abstention. A single coverage number hides a 0.183 subgroup gap.
   This is the core risk of deploying conformal prediction without
   selective abstention in heterogeneous populations.

2. Actionability (width < 3.0) is the most clinically relevant metric.
   At baseline, 32.5% of predictions are too wide to act on (width > 3.0).
   At 30% abstention, only 3.6% of accepted predictions exceed the threshold.
   The model is not making "better" predictions — it is making predictions
   only on the cases where it can be useful.

3. Global q cannot selectively improve. The comparison table shows
   global coverage = 0.930 constant at every abstention rate, global
   width = 6.875 constant. A non-adaptive model cannot distinguish easy
   from hard test cases. Locally adaptive intervals are a prerequisite
   for meaningful selective prediction.

4. The variance model is well-estimated (rho = 0.965). This is why the
   curve is clean — the uncertainty signal is real. In deployment, a
   degraded variance model would produce a flatter, noisier curve with
   lower actionability gains. The quality of the variance estimate directly
   determines the quality of the abstention decision.
